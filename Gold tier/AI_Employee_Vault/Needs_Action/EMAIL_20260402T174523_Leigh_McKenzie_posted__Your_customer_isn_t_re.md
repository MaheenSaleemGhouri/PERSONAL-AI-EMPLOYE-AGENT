## Body

Leigh McKenzie posted: Your customer isn't researching anymore. They're approving decisions an AI agent already made.

Someone asks ChatGPT to find a project management tool. Budget under $15 per seat. Needs time tracking and client dashboards. The agent evaluates six platforms, reads reviews, checks pricing, compares features, and starts a free trial.

The person gets a summary. Scans it. Approves.

They became aware of your brand at the moment they approved the purchase. The agent compressed awareness, consideration, and conversion into seconds.

Crystal Carter at Wix calls this a validation layer. Your customer used to weigh options. Now they're confirming decisions already made on their behalf.

And when the agent gets it right a few times? The review gets lighter. Trust builds. The person stops checking as closely.

This creates a new requirement for brands. Make it easy for agents to understand what you offer, verify it, and act on it. Two brands offer similar products. The one whose site lets agents parse structured data, confirm claims, and complete actions wins. The one that requires scraping and guessing gets passed over.

Specificity matters more than it used to. Salesforce built dedicated pages for automotive, healthcare, financial services, and retail. When an agent searches "CRM for automotive dealerships," Salesforce's automotive page speaks that language directly. Industry-specific features, case studies from automotive companies, use cases for that buyer.

Patagonia prompts customers to note height and activity in reviews. Every review becomes data an agent can match to a specific person. "Durable hiking pants for someone 5'10" who does scrambling" gives an agent exactly what it needs to recommend confidently.
